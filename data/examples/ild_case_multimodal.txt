Patient is a 67-year-old man with progressive exertional dyspnea and dry cough for two years.
He denies fever, hemoptysis, Raynaud phenomenon, inflammatory arthritis, sicca symptoms, and skin
rash. He is a former smoker with 25 pack-years. No known family history of pulmonary fibrosis.

Exposure and medication history: He kept pigeons at home for more than ten years but stopped six
months ago. He reports intermittent mold exposure in a damp basement. No amiodarone, nitrofurantoin,
immune checkpoint inhibitor, or thoracic radiation exposure is reported.

HRCT chest: Bilateral lower-lobe and subpleural reticulation is present with traction bronchiectasis.
There is no definite honeycombing. Mild patchy ground-glass opacity is present but is not the dominant
abnormality. No profuse micronodules, lobular air trapping, or upper-lobe predominance is described.
The radiologist's impression is probable UIP pattern.

Pulmonary function tests from 2026-02-12: FVC is 62% predicted, TLC is 68% predicted, and DLCO is
41% predicted. Compared with testing one year earlier, FVC declined from 74% to 62% predicted.

Laboratory tests: ANA is negative, RF is negative, anti-CCP is negative, and myositis antibody panel
is negative. CRP is mildly elevated. Peripheral eosinophil count is normal.

Clinician assessment: The pulmonologist notes fibrotic ILD with probable UIP pattern. Given the bird
and mold exposure history, chronic hypersensitivity pneumonitis remains in the differential, although
the HRCT description lacks classic air trapping or centrilobular nodules. IPF is also considered.
Multidisciplinary discussion is recommended.
